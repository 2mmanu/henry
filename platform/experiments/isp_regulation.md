# HR Hiring and Salary Regulations

## **Introduction**
This document outlines the policies and procedures for hiring and determining salaries at Intesa Sanpaolo. The goal is to ensure fair and competitive compensation practices that attract and retain top talent while maintaining internal equity and compliance with relevant laws and regulations.

---

## **Hiring Process**

### **1. Job Posting and Recruitment**
- All job openings must be posted on the company's career portal and other relevant job boards.
- Recruitment efforts should aim to attract a diverse pool of qualified candidates.
- Internal candidates are encouraged to apply and will be given equal consideration.

### **2. Application Review and Screening**
- HR will conduct an initial review of applications to shortlist candidates based on qualifications and experience.
- Shortlisted candidates will be contacted for a preliminary phone interview.

### **3. Interviews**
- Selected candidates will undergo a series of interviews, including technical and behavioral assessments.
- Interview panels should include members from HR, the hiring department, and other relevant stakeholders.
- All interviewers must complete the company's interview training program.

### **4. Reference and Background Checks**
- Before making a job offer, HR will conduct reference and background checks to verify the candidate's qualifications and work history.
- Any discrepancies or concerns must be addressed before proceeding with the offer.

### **5. Job Offer**
- HR will prepare and extend a formal job offer, including details of the position, salary, benefits, and start date.
- Candidates are required to sign and return the offer letter to confirm their acceptance.

---

## **Salary Structure**

### **1. Salary Ranges**
- The company maintains salary ranges for each job level and position based on market data and internal benchmarking.
- Salary ranges are reviewed annually and adjusted as needed to remain competitive.

#### **Typical Salary Ranges:**
- **Junior Software Developer:** €30,000 - €45,000 per year
- **Software Engineer:** €50,000 - €70,000 per year
- **Senior Software Engineer:** €70,000 - €100,000 per year
- **Lead Software Engineer:** €90,000 - €120,000 per year
- **Principal Software Engineer:** €110,000 - €150,000 per year

### **2. Determining Starting Salaries**
- Starting salaries are determined based on the candidate's qualifications, experience, and the salary range for the position.
- Consideration is given to internal equity to ensure fair pay among employees in similar roles.
- HR and the hiring manager will collaborate to determine an appropriate starting salary.

### **3. Salary Increases**
- Annual performance reviews are conducted to evaluate employee performance and determine eligibility for salary increases.
- Salary increases are based on individual performance, market adjustments, and the company's financial performance.
- Exceptional performance may be rewarded with higher increases or bonuses.

### **4. Promotions and Salary Adjustments**
- Employees promoted to a higher-level position will receive a salary adjustment to reflect the new responsibilities.
- The new salary will be within the range for the new position and based on the employee's qualifications and performance.

---

## **Payment Rules**

### **1. Payroll Schedule**
- Employees are paid on a bi-weekly basis.
- Salaries are deposited directly into the employee's bank account.

### **2. Overtime and Extra Compensation**
- Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5 times their regular hourly rate for hours worked beyond 40 in a week.
- Exempt employees are not eligible for overtime pay but may receive extra compensation for exceptional work or projects.

### **3. Bonuses and Incentives**
- Performance-based bonuses are awarded annually based on individual and company performance.
- Employees may also be eligible for additional incentives, such as project completion bonuses or spot bonuses for exceptional contributions.

### **4. Benefits and Perks**
- In addition to salaries, employees receive a comprehensive benefits package, including health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.
- Additional perks may include flexible working hours, remote work options, and professional development opportunities.

---

## **Compliance and Equity**
- The company is committed to maintaining compliance with all relevant labor laws and regulations.
- HR regularly reviews compensation practices to ensure equity and fairness.
- Any concerns or discrepancies regarding pay must be reported to HR for investigation and resolution.
